SD.GOV

Government ServiceNow

South Dakota needed to modernize how citizens interacted with state government services. We delivered a ServiceNow-powered platform with custom UX, intuitive information architecture, and a mobile-first case management system.

Challenge

Citizens were navigating a fragmented digital ecosystem of legacy government portals — each with different logins, navigation patterns, and visual languages. Completing basic tasks required significant effort.

Objectives

Consolidate disparate government services into a unified, accessible digital experience. Implement single sign-on across all state services. Build a scalable design system for future service additions.

Role & Tools

Lead Experience Designer at Servos — responsible for UX strategy, IA, interaction design, and design system development. Collaborated with ServiceNow developers, state stakeholders, and an accessibility consultant.

Platform Highlights

Six pillars of the redesign

  • Custom UX — Purpose-built visual design system on top of ServiceNow, giving SD.GOV a distinct, trustworthy identity
  • Intuitive IA — Reorganized 200+ services into a logical taxonomy derived from citizen mental models, not government org charts
  • Single Sign On — Citizens create one account and access all state services without re-authenticating
  • Global Search — Intelligent search surfaces relevant services, forms, and information from a single query
  • Mobile Friendly Interface — Designed mobile-first with progressive enhancement for desktop
  • Case Management — Citizens can track the status of any submitted request or application in real time

Citizens shouldn't need to understand how government is organized to access government services.

Research

Card sorting & tree testing

We conducted card sorting sessions with 32 South Dakota residents across 4 demographic groups. The results revealed that citizens organize government services around life events — not agency names. This became the backbone of the new information architecture.

Tree testing validated the final taxonomy with an 89% first-click accuracy rate before any visual design was completed.
