Spotivity

Mobile App Team of 4

Spotivity connects teens and parents to afterschool activities — but users weren't returning to the platform. Our team was tasked with driving re-engagement without overhauling the existing framework.

Challenge

Three major issues: users didn't understand the Personality Insight Tool, users felt stuck at "Enroll Now," and the Explore page was difficult to navigate.

Objectives

Drive user re-engagement without major changes to the pre-existing framework. Reduce friction at the enrollment CTA. Refine existing features rather than replace them.

Role & Tools

UX Designer on a team of 4 — competitive research, user interviews, concept testing, wireframes, interaction design, prototype, usability testing, and stakeholder presentation. Tools: Figma, Sketch, Miro.

Research

Assessing the current state

Nine hybrid user interviews and usability tests revealed:

  • Users didn't understand the Personality Insight Tool — its purpose and value were unclear
  • Users felt stuck at "Enroll Now" — the CTA felt like a high-commitment, irreversible action
  • The Explore page was difficult to navigate — the location-based search didn't match users' mental models

The Problem

Accessibility and relevance for teen users

HMW: "Disengaged teen users need accessible and relevant activity details to make informed decisions about enrollment."

Concept Testing

Four concepts, real users

  • PIT (Personality Insight Tool) — New introduction + results screen. Users liked having suggested activities on the same screen.
  • SPOT Chatbot — In-app chatbot. Users liked the conversational interface.
  • Explore Redesign — Redesigned location-based search with comparison tool.
  • Filters — Redesigned homepage with improved category headers.

If the CTA is less of a commitment, it decreases apprehension and increases conversion rate.

Pivot

Shifting "Enroll Now" to "I'm Interested!"

The prioritization matrix ranked: (1) Enroll CTA, (2) Explorer/Map and home screens, (3) Filter and categories, (4) PIT tutorial and results.

Usability Testing

Four tasks, clear results

  • Completing the PIT — Value was clear when personality results linked to recommended activities
  • Applying Filters — Easier access made teens more likely to use filters
  • Exploring via Map — Teens value the map when integrated with filtered preferences
  • "I'm Interested!" CTA — Far less commitment, more confidence, higher conversion
