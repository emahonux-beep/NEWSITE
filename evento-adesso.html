Evento Adesso

Events Team of 2

As remote work normalized, event hosts needed a way to create, manage, and monitor events remotely. Evento Adesso was designed not just to replicate in-person engagement — but to measure and intentionally increase it.

Challenge

Virtual events suffered from passive attendance. Hosts had no reliable way to know if their content was landing, and existing platforms were built for laptops with no focus on event planning.

Objectives

Design a platform that increases and measures engagement to create a fun and exciting experience for remote event hosts and attendees.

Role & Tools

Co-designer with Kaelyn Hartley — competitive research, user interviews, concept testing, wireframes, prototype, and usability testing. Tools: Figma, Sketch, Invision, Miro.

Research

Starting with the right questions

70% of professionals work from home at least once a week. Existing platforms are primarily developed for laptops. The increase in remote event hosting has unlocked a large window of opportunity.

Competitive Analysis

Seven platforms, one clear gap

Coursehorse, Udemy, Eventbrite, Meetup, Splash, Instagram, and Twitch were analyzed. None focused on the planning side of remote events.

User Interviews

11 participants, one clear insight

Key findings from 11 event hosts:

  • Hosts need ways to interact and engage — through discussions, polls, and other tactics
  • Engagement is intentional — hosts foster it by creating audience awareness
  • Platform preference — users primarily engage on larger screens
  • Social media marketing is integral — event promotion lives outside the platform

"In person, you feel the energy in the room. Online, you're presenting into a void and hoping something lands." – Dr. Mike Campbell

Pivot

From replication to measurement

The updated HMW: "Remote event hosts need a versatile platform that increases and measures engagement to create a fun and exciting experience."

Concepts

Three divergent directions

  • Templated Events — Pre-structured formats with statistical monitoring
  • LiveStream Syncing — Platform with sharing and social features
  • Body Motion Detection — Camera-based motion tracking for interactive classes

Usability Testing

Six participants, positive results

  • Intuitive for colleagues — users felt their colleagues would easily understand the platform
  • Hamburger menu friction — static side navigation took too much screen space; users wanted a foldout nav
