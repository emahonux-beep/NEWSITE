Digital ID

Healthcare Express Scripts

In 2022, our organization launched digital ID cards to reduce print and mail costs. Adoption was low and engagement minimal. I led the UX strategy and redesign across responsive and native platforms — transforming a static SVG replica into a discoverable, functional, wallet-integrated identity experience that became one of the most visited areas in the member portal.

Challenge

The digital ID card was rendered as an SVG template with member data injected into it. Members could toggle between front and back — and that was the entire experience. No structured metadata, no copy functionality, no wallet integration, no clear navigation entry point. Low traffic and minimal engagement confirmed the core problem: members simply were not encountering the feature.

Objectives

Increase digital adoption to reduce operational costs. Redesign for real-world use cases rather than digital replication of a physical artifact. Establish a scalable identity foundation that could be leveraged across the organization.

Role & Tools

UX Strategy Lead — experience audits, competitive research, analytics analysis, use case mapping, interaction design across responsive web and native platforms. Paired product redesign with adoption strategy including navigation, communication campaigns, and pre-effective onboarding.

Starting Point

The business case was clear. The experience was not.

The business case was straightforward: increase digital adoption to reduce operational costs — a shift with the potential to save millions in print and mail expenses annually. But when we audited the existing experience, deeper issues emerged.

The digital ID card was a rendered SVG template. Members could toggle front and back. That was it. There was no structured metadata, no copy functionality, no wallet integration, no contextual education, and no clear navigation entry point.

Analytics confirmed our findings: low traffic, low engagement, minimal repeat usage. The ID card lived inside the Account section, making it nearly impossible to find unless members already knew exactly where to look.

The problem was not just usability — it was discoverability, trust, and context.

The feature existed. Members simply were not encountering it.

Reframing

From artifact to moment

Instead of redesigning a card, we reframed the challenge around a single question: when do people actually need their ID card?

We identified six core use cases: checking in at a provider, visiting a pharmacy, sharing information with a dependent, downloading for offline access, adding to a digital wallet, and accessing card information before benefits are effective.

This shifted the focus from the artifact to the moment — and unlocked a completely different design direction.

Research

Three inputs, one direction

Experience Audit: A full review of web and native flows identified rendering limitations, missing utility features, and IA weaknesses that compounded the discoverability problem.

Competitive Research: We evaluated digital ID implementations across health insurers, financial institutions, rewards ecosystems, and digital wallet integrations. The strongest experiences went beyond replication — they enhanced usability with structured data and wallet-first thinking.

Analytics: Low engagement pointed less to dissatisfaction and more to invisibility. The feature existed. Members simply were not finding it.

Design

Five decisions that changed the experience

01

Restore visual trust

Replaced the rendered SVG with actual card images — front and back. Visual parity with the physical card reinforced recognition and trust immediately.

Visual Design Brand Alignment
02

Add structured, actionable content

Introduced copyable member ID and group numbers, dependent listings, key contact information, and structured plan details. Members no longer needed to zoom or screenshot.

Information Architecture Content Strategy
03

Apple Wallet & Google Wallet integration

Turned the ID card from a page into a portable asset. Wallet integration aligned with modern digital behaviors and competitive expectations — the card went where members actually needed it.

Native Integration Interaction Design
04

Share & download

A streamlined share function enabled download, email or message sharing, and offline storage — supporting real-world access at the pharmacy counter or provider check-in.

Use Case Design Mobile
05

Primary navigation entry point

Added a dedicated ID card icon to primary navigation. Post-release, the majority of traffic shifted immediately to this new entry point. Discoverability was the unlock.

IA Navigation Design

Digitizing a physical artifact is not transformation. Designing for real-world behavior is.

Adoption Strategy

Beyond launch

We paired the product redesign with a digital communication campaign, onboarding education, and a pre-effective welcome experience that allowed members to access their ID card before coverage even began. The goal was not just to ship a feature — it was to shift behavior.

Scaling

From feature to framework

The impact extended beyond a single product surface. Our team is now leveraging the research findings, analytics insights, and interaction patterns from this work to align ID card experiences across the organization — standardizing interaction models, creating reusable design components, aligning wallet integration approaches, and establishing scalable content structures.

Instead of fragmented implementations across lines of business, we are building a unified identity framework. The work evolved from feature redesign to systems-level thinking.

Reflection

What this work reinforced

This project reinforced a core principle: digitizing a physical artifact is not transformation — designing for real-world behavior is.

By shifting from a static SVG to a discoverable, functional, wallet-integrated experience, and by laying the foundation for organizational alignment, what began as a cost-saving initiative became a product maturity milestone.
