Bon Voyage

Travel Solo Project

The travel planning market is saturated, yet no platform truly brings together discovery, booking, social sharing, and local guidance under one roof. Bon Voyage was designed to be that missing all-in-one solution — an itinerary builder that fits in your pocket.

Challenge

Users struggled to find affordable hidden gems and local experiences. The current market focuses on booking, leaving users unsatisfied and ill-prepared. Multiple apps were required just to plan a single trip.

Objectives

Set a hypothesis to find product-market fit, create positive feedback loops, and build a product experience based on targeted consumer understanding.

Role & Tools

Solo UX Designer — competitive strategy research, consumer interviews, concept testing, wireframes, prototype designs, visual design, interaction design, and usability testing. Tools: Figma, Sketch, Miro, Validately.

Domain Research

How people plan travel today

Social media is a huge player in the travel game and often decides what trips are trending for younger audiences.

  • Social media drives trends — influencers dictate destinations; yet social platforms have no native planning or booking capability
  • Mobility is critical — travelers prefer mobile apps to access information at their fingertips
  • Market is saturated but incomplete — no all-inclusive travel platform exists
  • Budget consciousness — people want eclectic and budget-friendly stays, not just the most-reviewed options

Competitive Analysis

Seven competitors, one clear gap

TripAdvisor, Kayak, Airbnb, TripIt, Yelp, Lonely Planet, and Facebook Groups were analyzed. A gap was identified in planning-centered travel apps.

  • TripAdvisor — Strong reviews, weak planning, no social layer
  • Kayak — Best-in-class booking, zero curated content
  • Airbnb — Great local context, accommodation-only
  • TripIt — Excellent itinerary management, no discovery

User Research

Meet Emma Harris

Emma is a 27-year-old technical recruiter from Brooklyn — budget-conscious, craving authentic local experiences, and frustrated by the inability to plan local excursions ahead of time.

The frugal traveler needs to budget and research a trip so that they can create an organized itinerary to feel comfortable in their travels.

Design Process

From sketch to high fidelity

Four concepts were explored: Trip Dashboard, Trip Itinerary, Curated Trip Suggestions, and Calendar of Upcoming Trips. Two style tiles were tested — the handwritten travel journal direction was selected over the sophisticated/airy direction.

Usability Testing

Five tasks, real feedback

  • Signing In — Familiar and expected, no friction
  • Onboarding — Interest selection felt natural and social
  • My Profile — Miles flown, countries visited resonated as personal milestones
  • Adding a Trip — Category-based organization mapped to how users think
  • Reserving an Activity — Pricing breakdown and confirmation were highlights

Final Takeaways

Lean + Agile UX in practice

User input is more valuable than competitive research and personal assumptions. The final design may look completely different from your original vision — and that's the process working correctly.
